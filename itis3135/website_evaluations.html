Website Evaluations

Good design is about clarity, consistency, and purpose.

CRAP stands for Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity. These four ideas are what make a website easy to read and visually appealing. Below are two examples of websites I looked at to see how they apply (or break) these principles.

Apple

Apple’s website is a strong example of clean, simple, and organized design. It has a clear structure, easy navigation, and a professional look that makes sense for its brand.

  • Contrast: Text and backgrounds are balanced and easy to read.
  • Repetition: Layout, fonts, and buttons stay consistent throughout every page.
  • Alignment: Everything follows a grid that keeps the page neat and focused.
  • Proximity: Related elements like product titles and prices are placed together.

Overall, Apple’s website shows a strong understanding of design principles and gives users a smooth experience.

User Inyerface

User Inyerface is meant to be confusing and uncomfortable, which makes it a perfect example of poor design. It breaks many of the CRAP principles and shows how bad layout and organization can frustrate users.

  • Contrast: Colors do not work well together and can make reading difficult.
  • Repetition: There is no clear structure or consistency between pages.
  • Alignment: Many items feel randomly placed with no visual order.
  • Proximity: Buttons, text, and forms are crowded together in confusing ways.

The website does a good job of showing how ignoring design rules can make a page frustrating to use.
